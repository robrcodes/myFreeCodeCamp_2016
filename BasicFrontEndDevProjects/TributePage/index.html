“ Do nothing which is of no use ”

- Miyamoto Musashi (1584 - 1645)

Legendary Japanese swordsman, Musashi, was a roaming samurai who won an exceptional 60 duels in his life. He was also known for being an excellent artist, sculptor, calligrapher, author, and founder of the two sword combat technique niten'ichi.

The above text, Do nothing which is of no use , is the English translation of a popular quote from one of his best known books - The Book Of Five Rings

The following is a timeline showing some of the most notable events in his life:

  • 1584, Musashi is born.
  • 1591, Musashi is taken and raised by his uncles after death of his father.
  • 1596, His first duel win, and kill.
  • 1604, He famously battles the house of Yoshioka in Kyoto. Exact details vary as to the number of combatants he bested.
  • 1607-12, Travels and defeats several experts in duels.
  • 1612, Runs a fencing school for a short period.
  • 1614-15, fought with Tokugawa Ieyasu's troops in the campaigns at Osaka Castle.
  • 1615-21, worked as a construction supervisor for Ogasawara Tadanao in Harima Province.
  • 1623, travels to Edo and adopts a second son Iori
  • 1626, first adopted son, Mikinosuke, commits seppuku (ritual suicide).
  • 1630, enters the service of Lord Hosokawa Tadatoshi.
  • 1633, begins to focus heavily on practicing the arts.
  • 1637, only documented evidence of battle service, played a major role in the Shimabara Rebellion.
  • 1641, he writes Hyoho Sanju-go.
  • 1643, lives as a hermit after migrating into Reigando.
  • 1645, Finishes writing Go Rin No Sho (The Book of Five Rings).
  • 1645, dies at the age of 61 from suspected thoracic cancer.
